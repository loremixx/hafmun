The Shadow Security Council (SSC)

Not every decision is newsworthy. Not every conflict is front-page news. Some wars are just as silent as they appear, existing in back offices, in isolated deserts, through keystrokes and drone videos. No name, no flag, no answer.

The Shadow Security Council exists to address the parts of global conflict the world is not meant to see. A committee in which delegates must grapple with what it means to have power and act without the power of visibility and what it means to alter someoneâ€™s life without leaving discernible physical evidence.

In this committee delegates won't simply debate policy; they will grapple with the moral implications of secrecy, the power with which unbounded operations creates a world for many actors, and the implications of creating outcomes that no one will admit responsibility for. When there are no rules, where do you draw the line?
